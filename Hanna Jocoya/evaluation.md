## Concerns / Questions to Ask

1. **No phone call experience.** The posting lists "making calls to US businesses" as a must-have. Hanna's background is entirely digital marketing. Worth asking directly if she's comfortable making phone calls in English to US businesses (banks, insurance, government agencies).
2. **Relatively short career (2+ years).** All professional experience is at one company (Carisle Media). While the depth is impressive - managing 10+ accounts across multiple disciplines - she hasn't worked in different organizational contexts or with different types of bosses.
3. **Marketing specialist vs. generalist VA.** Her skills are deep in digital marketing but narrower on the personal assistant side (scheduling, travel planning, product sourcing, phone calls). The role is a 50/50 split of business and personal tasks. How comfortable is she with the non-marketing personal tasks?
4. **Zapier/automation gap.** She's transparent about this and frames it as a learning goal. Given her tool proficiency elsewhere, she'd likely pick it up fast, but it's currently a gap.
5. **No mention of Loom, Notion, or Toggl.** Several of the communication and documentation tools from the posting aren't in her toolkit yet.

The main gap is on the personal assistant side. Phone calls to US businesses, travel planning, and product sourcing aren't in her experience. She's also transparent about not having Zapier/automation experience yet. However, her demonstrated ability to pick up new tools independently and her expressed desire to learn automation suggest she could close these gaps.

She's the kind of candidate who could meaningfully contribute to growing the consulting business's pipeline through marketing - not just execute tasks but actually build systems that generate leads and clients. The question is whether the personal task half of the role is a good fit for someone whose strengths and interests are clearly in marketing strategy.

**Recommended next step:** Interview to assess comfort with phone calls and personal assistant tasks. Ask about her willingness to handle the non-marketing work. If she's genuinely flexible, she's a strong hire. If she's really looking for a marketing-focused role, there may be friction.
